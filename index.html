The Travelling Salesman Problem is one of the most famous—and infamous—problems in computational mathematics: given a list of cities and the distance between each city and every other city, calculate the shortest route that visits each city and returns to the origin city.

There are two types of algorithms available to solve this problem: exact algorithms, which provide an optimal solution given enough running time, and approximating algorithms, which use heuistics to provide answers that are "good enough" for many inputs and with huge reductions in time complexity.
